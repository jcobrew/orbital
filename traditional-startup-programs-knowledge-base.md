# Traditional Startup Support Programs — Reference

*Compiled June 2, 2026. Companion to the residential knowledge base (`startup-support-programs-knowledge-base.md`). Database: `traditional-programs-data.json`. Both datasets are viewable on the same map/globe via the **Residential ⇄ Traditional** toggle.*

---

## 1. What "traditional" means here

This is the **other half** of the founder-support world — the part that does *not* require you to move into a house or relocate to a co-living space. Where the residential database is about *place* (a mansion, a campus, a city you move to), the traditional database is about a **program**: a fixed-length cohort that gives you money, mentorship, and a network, then sends you to a demo day.

A clean analogy: if a residency is a **monastery** (you move in, the environment changes you), a traditional accelerator is a **bootcamp** (you commute in for a defined term, drill hard, graduate). You usually keep living wherever you already live.

The category splits into a few recognizable shapes:

| Shape | What you get | Archetype |
|---|---|---|
| **Accelerator** | Small cheque + 3-month cohort + demo day, for equity | Y Combinator, Techstars |
| **Talent investor** | Money + co-founder matching *before* a company exists | Entrepreneur First, Antler |
| **Corporate accelerator** | Access to a big company's customers/pilots, often no equity | Plug and Play, Wayra |
| **Government accelerator** | Public grants/visa support to build in a country | Start-Up Chile, Hub71 |
| **Deep-tech / lab program** | Lab space + mentorship for science startups | IndieBio, HAX, CDL |
| **Startup campus** | A building hosting many programs at once | Station F |

> **Why this is a separate view.** Earlier we deliberately *removed* these from the residential map because they're not residencies — there's no live-in or relocation element. But they're the backbone of the global ecosystem, so they get their own database and their own toggle on the map rather than being deleted outright.

## 2. The economics (how to read the database)

The single most useful lens is **what they take and what they give**:

- **Standard-equity accelerators** give ~$125K–$500K and take ~6–7% (Y Combinator, 500 Global, Platanus, Iterative). The deal is deliberately commoditized.
- **Talent investors** give less (~$80–190K) for *more* equity (~8–12%) because they take the risk of backing a person with no company yet (EF, Antler).
- **No-equity programs** trade access for ownership: corporate accelerators (Plug and Play, Wayra), government programs (Start-Up Chile, MassChallenge, CDL). You give time/attention, not equity.
- **Deep-tech programs** give more cash *and* physical lab space because biology/hardware can't be built on a laptop (IndieBio ~$525K + wet lab; HAX $250K + hard-tech lab).

> **Technical note — "MFN SAFE" keeps showing up.** Most of these invest via a *SAFE* (a simple agreement that converts to equity later) with an *MFN* ("most-favored-nation") clause — meaning if you later raise on better terms, the accelerator automatically gets those better terms too. For your project the significance is that the *price* of these programs is often deferred and conditional, which makes "how much equity does it cost?" genuinely hard to compare across programs.

## 3. The global map (active programs only)

The database includes **only programs that are still operating** in 2026, deliberately spread across every region so the map isn't just a San Francisco story:

- **North America** — Y Combinator, Techstars, a16z Speedrun, 500 Global, Pear (PearX), Alchemist, LAUNCH, Founder Institute, AI Grant, Conviction Embed, Betaworks Camp, MassChallenge, Plug and Play, Sequoia Arc, IndieBio, HAX, gener8tor, and CDL (Toronto).
- **Europe** — Entrepreneur First (London), Seedcamp (London), Station F (Paris — the world's largest startup campus), Startup Wise Guys (Tallinn), APX (Berlin), Founders Factory (London), Wayra (Madrid).
- **MENA** — Hub71 (Abu Dhabi, with Techstars Hub71), Flat6Labs (Cairo + Riyadh/Abu Dhabi/Bahrain).
- **Africa** — MEST Africa (Accra + Lagos/Nairobi/Cape Town), Startupbootcamp AfriTech (Cape Town), Founders Factory Africa (Johannesburg).

- **Asia** — Peak XV Surge (Bangalore/SEA — up to $3M seed), Iterative (Singapore), Antler (Singapore + India).
- **Oceania** — Startmate (Sydney — the ANZ anchor).

## 4. How "active" was judged

A program is included only if there's current (2026) evidence it is **still running cohorts or accepting applications** — a live site, a recent cohort announcement, or fresh fund news. Recruiting *status* per program (rolling / open / closing-soon / closed) is a separate, faster-moving field shown on each pin; "closed" there means *this cohort's applications are closed*, not that the program is dead. Programs that have genuinely wound down (e.g. Pioneer, The Mint) are excluded entirely rather than marked closed.

> **Reminder to act:** application windows move weekly. If you're tracking any of these for the thesis or to apply, the three with the clearest near-term clocks are **a16z Speedrun** (SR007 just closed — next window later in 2026), **Platanus Ventures** (2026 calls open now), and **Techstars / Hub71** (rolling by program). Open the map's **Traditional** view, filter by "Open / rolling," and start there.
